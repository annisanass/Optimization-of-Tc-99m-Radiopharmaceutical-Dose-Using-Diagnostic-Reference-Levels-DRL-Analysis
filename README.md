# Optimization-of-Tc-99m-Radiopharmaceutical-Dose-Using-Diagnostic-Reference-Levels-DRL-Analysis

## Overview
This project evaluates radiopharmaceutical dose administration in nuclear medicine procedures by comparing typical administered doses with national Diagnostic Reference Levels (DRL). The study aims to assess dose optimization practices and ensure radiation safety compliance.

## Objective
To analyze and evaluate the administered activity (MBq) in several nuclear medicine procedures and compare them with Indonesian DRL (I-DRL) standards to identify potential optimization opportunities.

## Methods
- Data collection from multiple nuclear medicine procedures (2022–2024)
- Calculation of typical administered doses (MBq)
- Comparison with national DRL values (I-DRL)
- Evaluation of compliance and dose optimization

## Procedures Analyzed
- Myocardial Perfusion Scan (SPM) – Tc-99m MIBI  
- SPM Viability – Tc-99m MIBI  
- Bone Scan – Tc-99m MDP  
- GFR & Renogram – Tc-99m DTPA  
- Whole Body Scan (WBS) – Tc-99m MIBI  
- Thyroid Scan – Tc-99m Pertechnetate  

## Key Results
- *SPM (2023–2024):* Above DRL → indicates potential overexposure  
- *SPM Viability:* Within DRL range → acceptable  
- *Bone Scan (2022–2024):* Below DRL → optimized  
- *GFR & Renogram:* Within DRL → appropriate  
- *WBS Scan:* Below DRL → optimized  
- *Thyroid Scan:* Slightly above DRL (2023), improved in 2024  

## Impact
The findings highlight variability in administered doses across procedures. Some procedures exceed DRL values, indicating the need for optimization, while others demonstrate effective dose management. This study supports radiation protection principles and quality assurance in nuclear medicine.
